WHAT IS NRW?

National Robotics Weekend (NRW), organized by IEEE RAS INSAT, is Tunisia’s exclusive robotics congress, held annually in June in Tunis. Attracting around 350 students, NRW is a unique event that provides a dynamic platform for Tunisia’s future engineers. Participants have the choice to either compete in various challenges and competitions or enrich their knowledge through state-of-the-art workshops and enlightening keynotes. The event is dedicated to uniting the robotics community, nurturing new connections, inspiring innovation, and enhancing technological skills across diverse fields.

WHAT IS IEE RAS INSAT?

IEEE RAS INSAT SBC, the Robotics and Automation Society Student Branch Chapter, serves as the organizing unit for the national robotics weekend. It's one of the largest and most dynamic chapters within IEEE, its primary objective is to foster innovation and advance research in the fields of robotics and automation. With a membership exceeding 200 individuals, it consistently delivers engaging activities and initiatives in order to cultivate interest and expertise in these fields.

It is one of the most active Student Branch Chapter in Tunisia Playing a vital role at INSAT ecosystem by:

  • Organizing The ONLY robotics congress in Tunisia: National Robotics Weekend
  • Representing Tunisia Yearly in EUROBOT since 2022
  • Organizing RAS ODYSSEY: a Program to Teach 100 students embedded systems, robotics and AI in 5 Sessions for each domain since 2024.
  • Organizing InnoWeek: A week of intensive learning and innovation in embedded systems, culminating in a spirited showcase of skills at INSAT. (2024)
  • Arranging “RoboCraft” was a successful series where participants built autonomous robots, with attending the Esprit Ras Robots competition.

NETWORKING AND EXHIBITION

National Robotics Weekend not only fosters innovation but also cultivates a community through its networking and exhibition opportunities. The event's job fair and student project exhibition are pivotal for establishing connections and showcasing talent.

To enhance these activities, consider incorporating:

  • Interactive Panels: Invite industry leaders to discuss emerging trends in robotics, providing attendees with insights into the future of the field.
  • Mentorship Sessions: Pair students with professionals for one-on-one mentorship, offering guidance on career development within the robotics sector.
  • Innovation Challenges: Host mini-competitions during the exhibition, encouraging participants to solve real-world problems presented by partner companies.
